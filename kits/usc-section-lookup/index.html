What this is

A time-travel lookup engine for US statutory text. Every result is verbatim primary source — no model-generated summary, no inference, no paraphrase. The text you see is the text Congress enacted, in the edition year you select, SHA-256 verified against the source PDF.

⚖ Why lawyers use this
  • Date-of-conduct statutory text. "What did §42:300gg say on the date my client acted?" is the foundational liability question. Westlaw charges premium rates for historical versioning. This answers it in seconds, free.
  • Repeal defense. If a section was removed before your client's conduct, there is no statutory duty. See exactly when any section disappeared from the code.
  • ACA onset. §42:18001 et seq. first appear in the 2012 edition. Pre-2012 conduct has no ACA exposure. Verifiable here in one lookup.
  • Congressional intent — first class. Enacted findings (the "★ Congressional findings" flag) are Congress's own stated purpose, embedded in the statute. Courts treat this as primary evidence of legislative intent. No legislative history database required.
  • Edition-to-edition text drift. The character-count compare bar shows when a section was substantially rewritten — a signal to read the diff carefully before citing.
🛡 How this avoids the sanctions risk
Rule 11 & Rule 3.3 exposure from AI-generated legal research is real. Courts have already sanctioned attorneys for citing hallucinated cases.
  • No LLM in the result path. What you see is the exact text extracted from the official govinfo.gov PDF for that edition year. No model touched it. No summary was generated. The text is the text.
  • SHA-256 provenance chain. Every corpus release ships a MANIFEST_SHA256.txt. The hash of the source PDF that produced this text is recorded. You can verify it against the live govinfo.gov file — a reproducible chain of custody you can show your work with. (For filing, always cite and verify against the official USC.)
  • Caveats are schema fields, not footnotes. The footer caveat travels with every export: snapshot_year_is_not_enactment_date. This is accurate — use official USC for filing. This tool is your research compass, not your citation.
  • Absence is documented. If a section is not in the corpus, the tool says so explicitly. It does not hallucinate a result. A blank card means not indexed — not "not law."

⚖ For Counsel — three things to know before the demo
I
The sources are the sources. This corpus is built from primary sources — official USC editions published by the Office of Law Revision Counsel, the U.S. Statutes at Large, and Harvard Law School's Caselaw Access Project (~1.8M federal opinions). The AI does not generate law. It indexes law that already existed and makes it queryable across five decades. Every record links back to its authoritative origin.
II
"Milspec" means reproducible under adversarial conditions. Every query result is tied to a SHA-256-verified corpus snapshot. The same query against the same snapshot always returns the same result — the way a certified copy of a transcript is the same as the original. If this system's output were ever needed in a proceeding, the exact corpus version, the exact model weights, and the exact inputs can be produced and re-run by opposing counsel on their own hardware.
III
BitNet is deterministic. The 2-billion-parameter model scoring this corpus is weight-attested — its parameters are hash-pinned at a specific version. Deterministic inference means: same model + same input + same seed = identical output, every time, by anyone. It is not a black box. It is a signed, reproducible calculation. The 72-billion-parameter cross-check (Qwen) confirms or disputes each score — disagreements are the finding, not an error.
🧠 How the corpus audits itself — the two-model disagreement surface

Every section in this corpus is independently scored by two AI models from different architectural families — a 2-billion-parameter military-spec model (BitNet) and a 72-billion-parameter general model (Qwen). They score each section on the same two questions:

★ Does this section contain a proclaimed WHY?
Congressional findings, purpose clauses, legislative intent statements embedded in the statute itself. Courts treat this as primary evidence of legislative intent — no legislative history database required.
⚡ Is this section's operative status honestly classifiable?
Is it clearly operative, clearly repealed, or genuinely ambiguous from the text alone? Some sections are zombies — technically on the books but functionally hollow. Others are contested territory.
Both models agree
Corroborated verdict. High confidence. Green signal in the corpus — use with normal legal judgment.
Models disagree
This IS the finding. The section is in the ambiguity surface — the territory where even independent AI systems cannot resolve the question. That is exactly where human legal judgment matters most. The corpus flags it rather than hiding it.

This is not AI replacing legal judgment. It is AI mapping the edges of legal certainty — so you know which sections reward a second read and which are settled ground. The disagreement surface is currently being computed across all 2,500 section-slots (500 per edition × 5 editions). Results populate as the cross-check run completes.
