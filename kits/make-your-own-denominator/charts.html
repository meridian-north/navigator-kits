The system removes problem vaccines — when it has the data

When post-market surveillance caught a real, identifiable signal, the system acted: suspended, replaced, or de-recommended the product. These are sharp, identifiable signals. The subtler, diffuse, product- or lot-specific questions are exactly what a denominated, linked system would let anyone check. Pro-safety, not anti-vaccine — and an argument for opening the data, not closing it.

2 · Why our null is honest about what it can't see (the anti-placebo limit)

Our schedule check compares each vaccination age to its adjacent days. That catches a sharp post-shot spike — but on the dense infant schedule the neighbor days aren't a true placebo, so a diffuse effect lifts them too and gets subtracted out. Left: a sharp spike stands above its neighbors (caught — this is how rotavirus/intussusception flags). Right: a diffuse rise hides inside the comparison (missed). Beating this needs a true unvaccinated baseline — the withheld linked data.
