Make your own denominator

You are handed a numerator and denied a denominator — on purpose. Here is the problem made visible, a test of whether the method even works, the method run on the contested question, and the one decision that resolves it.

Neutral on safety · strong on method · a tool, not a verdict. Counts of reports are not rates; a correlation is not a cause.

→ See the evidence charts — the vaccine-removal record, the anti-placebo limit, the "1932 vs 404" fraction, and the cost of slow surveillance (Vioxx vs Pandemrix).

1 · The missing number

"Three children died." A lot, or nothing? You cannot say — until you know the "out of how many." That bottom number is the denominator. A numerator without it is not a fact you can judge; it is a number floating in the dark.

VAERS — the open vaccine report box — can only ever give a numerator: how many reports came in, never out of how many people, and never how many of those events would have happened anyway. So both scary directions of headline are unsupported: "thousands of reports!" and "no proven harm!" alike. The box was never built to carry a denominator. The honest reply to "is it safe?" is "that is answerable, and we are structurally prevented from answering it."

2 · The denominator gap, made visible

For four groups of children: deaths reported to VAERS within a week of a shot (an undercounted floor), against the deaths ordinary background mortality alone would produce in that same week — our broad estimate, drawn as a range. The range is wide on purpose: it is wide because the real denominator (doses by age and week) is withheld. Release it and every grey bar collapses to a line.

The dare: the grey estimate is deliberately broad, and on every plausible denominator its low end still towers over what was reported — that is what "no gross excess" looks like on the open record. There is exactly one way to prove it wrong: release the actual denominator. The width of these bars is not ours to fix. It is theirs to collapse. Why don't we have it?

3 · Does the method even work? A positive control

A tool that can only find problems is propaganda; a tool that finds the real one and clears the false ones is an instrument. So we point the same observed-vs-expected engine at two known, accepted signals, at two different ages — quantified by the FDA's own Mini-Sentinel/PRISM system and CDC's Vaccine Safety Datalink: rotavirus → intussusception (~2 months), and MMR/MMRV → febrile seizures (12 months).

4 · The same method on the contested question — 19 years, no model

Now the same observed-vs-expected logic on the real U.S. linked infant-death files — all-cause, by single day of age (the coding-immune axis: age is date arithmetic, not a label that can drift). And here we hold ourselves to the strictest form: no model, no smoothing, no threshold, and years not pooled. We do not fit a curve (a fit would assume the decline's shape); we compare each vaccination-age window only to its own immediately-adjacent equal-width windows of raw days. The one question with no assumptions: does the death count at the vaccination age exceed BOTH neighbors? On a normal declining curve it won't; a real post-shot bump would — and would repeat across years.

We ran 19 years of real data (1995–2013 and 2023; 2002 auto-excluded by a built-in quality check — its public-use file carries an age-coding artifact, flagged openly rather than hidden). Vaccines and the schedule changed across these years (whole-cell DTP→DTaP, thimerosal removed ~2001, Hib/PCV/rotavirus added, COVID only recently), so we deliberately do not average the years — a formulation- or lot-specific problem would surface as one standout year, which pooling would erase.

At 2 and 4 months the vaccination-age window never once exceeded both its neighbors across 19 years; at 6 months it did in a single year (chance-level), with median ratios sitting at or just below 1. No constant schedule-timing effect, and no standout year of the kind a bad formulation or lot would produce. It does not prove safety — all-cause dilutes a rare cause-specific signal, the window is a timing test, and it cannot name a product. That last limit is the whole point: attributing any year would need the linked vaccination records (product, lot, date) — the withheld denominator.

5 · The credibility pair

Same engine. Two honest outcomes: it catches the real signal when linked data exists (§3), and it returns an honest null on the contested question (§4). That signal was only ever found because a few researchers had linked individual data (FDA Mini-Sentinel, CDC VSD) the public cannot reproduce. The method is not the bottleneck — it is open and proven. The data is.

6 · The ask — bounded, non-partisan

1. Open the denominated, linked data — vaccination dates and outcome dates by age — in the de-identified, date-shifted form already used internally, so any qualified party can run the same engine and get the same answer.

2. Require signal analyses to be reproducible: published code, pinned data versions, hashes — a result checked, not asserted.

3. Fund the open rebuild of automated EHR-based surveillance — the ESP:VAERS engine that was built and proven in 2011, then shelved — as a public utility.

This is not a demand to expose anyone's records. The engine needs dates and ages, not identities. Some things deserve secrecy — a person's identity does. A nation's vaccine-safety denominator does not. The barrier is permission, not privacy.
